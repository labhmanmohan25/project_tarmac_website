## WHAT CHANGED AND WHY

The old deck pitches a consumer travel app for group trips.
The new deck pitches a B2B SaaS tool for independent travel advisors.

**Old thesis:** "Travelers need a trip-planning AI."
**New thesis:** "Travel advisors run $100B in bookings on Word docs and spreadsheets. That ends now."

The new audience is angels and pre-seed VCs. The message must answer 3 questions in the first 60 seconds:
1. What's the problem, and why does it matter?
2. How big is the market?
3. Why is this team the one to build it?

## COPY PRINCIPLES USED IN THIS DECK

These guided every word choice — keep them when adding slides later:

1. **One problem per slide.** Not three. Not five. One.
2. **The Uber rule:** State the problem like it's obvious. "Cabs are unreliable and expensive." Not: "The urban ground transportation market exhibits significant inefficiencies in pricing and reliability."
3. **Numbers anchor everything.** Every claim has a number. Every number has a source.
4. **Short sentences.** If a sentence needs a comma, it might be two sentences.
5. **Never say "leverage," "synergy," "disrupt," or "revolutionize."**
6. **The investor reads in 3 minutes.** Every slide must work as a standalone image.
7. **Lead with what they lose, not what they gain.** Loss aversion is stronger than opportunity framing.
